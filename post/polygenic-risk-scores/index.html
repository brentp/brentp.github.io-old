Polygenic Risk Scores

2018-08-14 149 words 1 min read

Recently, the Nature Genetics letter Genome-wide polygenic scores for common diseases identify individuals with risk equivalent to monogenic mutations got a lot of attention, including in the New York Times. Here are some things I notice about the paper. I am happy to have any corrections to my thinking as comments/tweets/ or whatever.

Summary

The authors look at summary stats (p-values and betas/odd-ratios) for 5 phenotypes: coronary artery disease (CAD), atrial fibrilation (AF), type 2 diabetes, inflammatory bowel disease, and breast cancer. They use a “validation set” of ~120K people and a “test set” of 289K people of British ancestry. They test 25 or so thresholds/parameter combinations to find the p-value and LD cutoffs for variants to use in the risk score for each phenotype. For each phenotype they show an increase in prevalence at the high GPS scores derived from the optimal cutoff for that phenotype.
